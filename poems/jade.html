Jade Without Light

A whisper, silently floating through me, Untold, unheard — for others to see. Observer — that's what I was in that space, Like nobody could feel me or see my face. A jade-like beauty, without her own light, She shine with others — different in the night. A heretic in brightness, Alone in a cold, dark silence. A bird she was — butchered by right, A pool of blood, a knife in sight. She tried to fly, precious wings torn — A cold stare, a bloody night — a lipstick of light. A drop of rain — the singularity of me. One second I saw... the next, I ceased to be. A god's dream, unable to escape the pain, Letting go of the world I had created — just for me.
- Gaurab Aryal
