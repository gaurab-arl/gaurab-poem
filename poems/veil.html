Veil

A veil all girls wear, Like bonnets hiding Their tender faces. A warm silk thread, Fine piece of cloth— Wrapped 'round a goddess's gaze And sacred air. A sweet, fine poison— She spoiled the silence When she spoke. Like wine, she slipped away. The veil was lifted, The mask removed— Once your hiding place. And what did I saw? An old face. Yet, Beneath it all, A real person lingered. So aged you seemed, With a soul of dying flame. New to strangers, Old to the old— I think now, You were never gold. Just silver— Yet I hold to you, Like a tale Where an axe is cast Into a wind-blown stream. As soft as you were, As thorny as you grew— I’d never seen that side. Even though it was you, It hurt me. To have you— A blessing, Perhaps a goddess by my side. But you’d leave me On my worst days, Return only when I’d stitched my wounds. What can I say to you? Just one truth you must know: You kept spamming my thoughts— And still, I was never bored. Because that was the only time You truly saw.
- Gaurab Aryal
