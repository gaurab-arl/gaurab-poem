Mother

A deeply emotional poem reflecting on a child's transformation, guilt, and realization of a mother's silent strength.

The child I became, The person she made, The devil I turned into— Stil with her all care. I pray to demons, She prays to God. A change began in me As I started to fall. The devil Helped me descend, But as she prayed to God, He pitied seeing my fall. What’s left in me? I am lost. The feeling of loss Occurs in when I’m alone— Like a breeze, like a weight, I begin to free fall. The room I was kept in Is now filled with water— I can’t see a thing. I was never meant to drown, But I still try to swim. The things I ignored, Calling them small, Now I do them. All of them feels like, Flood, Comming my way, I seems to realize, The pain you felt. I now feel pressure. The smile never left me As I took on your work. Now I understand The helplessness you hid. The pain of losing— I feel it now. Lost in memories, I feel so small. I was a fool To not see the vast world You were shielding me from. What is the hope You see in me? Is it love? Or a dream You wish me to fulfill? The child inside me Looks out at the world— He sees wonder in everything. But I see boredom in it. Now I see the adult me Is dying slowly— From overthinking. The child I neglected once Is calling me. The small child playing beside me Sees a girl— The one who gave me life. She was never young, Yet never seemed old. As I continue to grow, I begin to show The cracks you once filled— Now they only grow. The face I hated, You loved so much. You saw a world in me, I saw only dust. What’s the fault in me? you ask— I am fault, And fault is me. What will be left of me When I see you Leaving?
- Gaurab Aryal
