Hospital

The hospital ,I visited Filled with many insects Looks like people’s Like ants swarming everywhere. I saw so much. Watched quietly. Different people, With different wounds, Each story held in silence. Short-lived beauty never moved me. The pain I suffer Unspoken, unbearable— Cannot be tolerated much longer. Old seems fine Just last moment remains Young seems to be devastated By their negligence. Just a small thing, just a spark— becomes a fire we never expected. Now we try to fix , Something With only last breath remains. Like ants looking for food, People look into others, Searching for answers. Maybe they have felt relieved After seeing other conditions. What are we waiting for? A cure? If it can’t be found, then some would rather burn it all. I never told anyone Expect me, What the pain I am Suffering. I don’t want to say it aloud. I think it's regret— This shadow is feeling. Pain has never felt so real, so sharp. I never want to feel it again. I just wanted sleep. But I knew my time was coming— quietly, without warning. I never prepared. Only hid the truth, so I wouldn’t seem so weak. A bird has two wings. I am one— infected, sick. The other still flies, but the sickness is spreading. We just don’t see it. This situation is terrible. I fear what lies ahead, The consequences  We yet to face. The hospital is like a game— We move with worry, No map, No menu, Just NPCs commanding, And we, the players, Follow blindly. What do I say now? I am not a doctor. I am a patient— Unaware That pain erased My motivation. I’ve seen people— So many. Each with their own burdens, None lighter than mine. Some cry, Some faint. It is a haunting beauty This hospital makes. Patients come and go— a daily rhythm. But the suffering? Not chronic disease— it’s the negligence they feed themselves Always dreaming Impossible things, Like me fly In sky, Fall with greater hight, Then dies.
- Gaurab Aryal
