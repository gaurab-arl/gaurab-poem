Hell

Censure of white haze— Feel that gaze, Staring down a prey. Just one gulp, And it’s the end. Floating pieces—masses— Lifeless. A person far away, beside someone Still breathing, still alive— But it feels like a game. I ride a moving train, Away from place. What’s the starting point? My death. Complain, complain— Complications. Call a doctor, look at me: This leg, this body— Bones broken, Popping like a debate. A creepy voice From a gentle girl, With a smiling face: “Haha... is that you, My love— Still waiting for peace in a HELL?” I killed her. From my eyes, she saw inside— A portal, a gateway, To somewhere— A beautiful place. The one I’ll go to, once I die. A board hung above the door, Carved in flame: “WELCOME TO HELL, MY DEAREST.” A sin of pride, A sin of lust— All seven sins Welcome me, heart in hand. Ask me—whose hearts? They’re theirs. I am their leader, Their liberator. Follow my lead. Follow my path. Let’s walk into fire, Burn all in sight— Child, young, old, woman or man. This is my realm, The place where I rule Don’t call anyone innocent— All are demons, Wearing flesh But forged from flame. A enternal flames Not even a child is spared All have commited sins Let me burn them in my place.
- Gaurab Aryal
