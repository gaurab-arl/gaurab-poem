Inspiration or Parasite

Are you an inspiration — or a parasite? Like still water, muddy and unclear. Each time it ripples, it shifts — What stays the same? Do tides ever return as they were? I relate to things that never existed. Winds pass me by, avoiding my way. I chase them — like a child after butterflies, While you soar, a bird that always flies away. A crimson moon in the sky, glowing bright. A lonely night — I wander past midnight. At eleven o’clock, I walk down empty roads, And in the silence... I find something I enjoy. Was that a cry yesterday? Or empty tears? An emptiness shared, joy that split apart. The one who crafted such technique — Was someone who never received what was his: Disillusion. What’s left in the past — Maybe just memory. Or the kindness you showed, Never whispering a word — Only silence,Only effort… Some things were fated — Until someone gave away.
- Gaurab Aryal
