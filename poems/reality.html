Reality

Every time I think how a boy is different from a girl, I wonder— what’s the difference in thinking? How can I get to those feelings? Where do I search if I don’t even have a clue? Two girls talked, one in front of the mirror, and I was just a listener— far from their place, where they couldn’t see me. They talked about a boy— maybe thinking about me. Or maybe that's just the delusion in me. What can I say about it? It was just a normal talk, like how boys would talk. But with every word, someone’s dream silently shattered. Is what you said the truth? You’re so jealous of things when someone speaks to you— then turns away. Haha… What could I say? Words left me in a precious moment. Confusion embraced me. What can I expect from others if I got a different version of you from someone else? Your fear— it let me console you. But why give life for something you call nonsense if I already gave you mine? What were you thinking, like an innocent child I never saw before? Why are you so open to your mirror self? All your secrets— known. Nothing hidden. Is that the key to a real relationship? I never knew you lived alone. The responsibility you carry is greater than mine. But I realized it far too late to follow. Watching from a distance— that’s all I ever had. I was never worthy. Just realized something crucial only a day ago. The qualities you spoke of— it left me in trouble. Why even look at requirements if I was never qualified to begin with? The wrong things said— they may be true. But not everyone thinks like you. Well, well— as expected from you. You never disappoint me. It’s just that the disappointment was always me. The other version of you— the one in the mirror— she’s so different from you. What’s together or single if there’s present love? We can’t live in distance, I see that now. Haha… Just tell me— I’ll buy a castle It will be a gift. Ten minutes feels like centuries. I’m growing old, just like you. But where will home be if we disappear from each other, never willing to move together? I never knew family requirements mattered in searching. Seems I wasn’t even qualified to apply. The thought of never marrying, never having a child— it’s the same for me. Two sides of the same coin with different personalities. You talk of marriage, yet one of you is child, and the other is older than real thing. but you see a brother in me. Is that why you never liked me? The boy you were talking about— why does it feel like it’s me? Haha… Just tell me. Because when I think deeply, I know— it surely isn’t. What was the talk you had with your reflection? The mirror-you hated physical touch… yet I touched it. So is the poison growing in you meant for me? The you I see loves it. What do I say now? You left me in thought— so deeply. The softening words the mirror-you spoke, said to live in comfort, while you told me. When I think of you, I think of the boyfriend you once left to see. The frustration— the other voices you talked to and will continue to talk to— leaves me feeling betrayed. The life of a boy— it’s not so easy, you see. We try to help, but expect nothing. Still— the pain lingers. I helped out, but no one appreciated it. Why did I help? For the feeling of happiness? Helping others and wanting help in return are very different feelings. We gave up our peace so you could live comfortably, while we already sat with betrayal in our hearts. The last seat— me and my friend— I see still. You were outside, Of me. and we were lost in something. The worry your beloved felt when you left— it was the same as mine. I already knew… but I never tried to improve. You see boys as dirt. Maybe you think I’m the same. But that’s a misunderstanding. I think good of you. As a god, I would serve— if you'd bless me with your feelings. My mind is corrupted with overthinking and expectating. Now I think you overthink too. Maybe I seemed like I didn’t care, didn’t want to be involved. But trust me— just say a word, and I’ll appear right in front of the mirror you talk to. If you want anything— just tell me. I’m here to help you in need. But maybe, I’m not even a friend you’d consider. Just a random person who shows up when you are at peace.
- Gaurab Aryal
