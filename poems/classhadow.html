Shadows of Classmates

I walked the road of many shadows—but I was the darkest of them all. Swimming through floods of thought, drowning in whispers I heard. Some never spoke, bound by a secret agreement. Yet, all seemed to know. What’s the use of that show? A powerful force divided. I didn’t want to tell. I didn’t want to remember. All seemed so… A twisted web trapped —some were like mirrors, some rivers, some illusions sharp enough to turn the spine to silver. Delusions so real they breathed. A demon lurked behind an innocent face, a jealous eyes, ear watching, waiting. Every meaning lost its taste though happy feeling. How much to see? How much to feel? I was a sailor who had lost his way, searching for peace in a storm without end. The rising sun set too quickly. Days blurred together, all the same—yet the Visitor was different. A definition shaped in silence, a truth that fractured by person. In the end, we all drowned. …But some were saved who already know the way.
- Gaurab Aryal
