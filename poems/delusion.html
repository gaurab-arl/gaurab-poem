Delusion

Delusion created by us.

Delusion I see, But others feel. Talked to a guy I met, Don’t know how funny things were, But I enjoyed and learned from him. A family of three I saw— Mother, father, child. Yet I’m still finding me. These are theoretical things, Expressed by people around me, Except me. Husband and wife, but child different. “Your husband said to me,” I didn’t understand you then— Now I see. Didn’t expect the things My friend said to me. I don’t wish it to be me. A friend asked another friend, “Want a wife?” He named a girl I know. I looked at him. His answer was unexpected, Least expected by me. What can I say? Words escape me. It’s the same story again, A husband once told me. An interesting tale unfolds— I shared it with a friend. He asked a girl for a kiss, She said yes, you see. But no action followed— He was scared to commit. I feel. I asked another friend beside me, “Would you try it?” He said no, Didn’t want a small thing to be, Didn’t want it. A very dark reality. A festival came, his friend, you see, Talked to girls a month before. For a time, It was a conspiracy Like a water pouring in sand Then they vanished, Like never seen. It was a normal things, Every time happening. But how the girls fell? The girls saw it as normal, but I didn’t I asked him, “Do you have experience?” About a love He shared a bitter story, I didn’t want to share it. Then I Listened and realized— how a child was lost Due to people’s fearing something. I fell from the sky, Didn’t expect that side to be. Now everything is haunting. A group of people traveling together, The reality I see. “How many are you with?” A girl asked a friend beside me. “Don’t misunderstand,” A girl said to another friend I was with. It was just a question. All seems to be joking, Question were answered with embarrassment by all Expect me Now it was a girl turn to answer it She answered, “Two aside from one, I see.” Jumping from the building— Is that what I feel? Please leave me alone for a bit.
- Gaurab Aryal
